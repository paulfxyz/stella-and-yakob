The Story

What is AI?
And why does it matter?

One grey evening, Stella overhears her parents speaking in worried voices about something called "artificial intelligence." Her father might lose his job. Her mother cried. Stella doesn't understand — and that knot in her chest is real.

Then Yakob arrives: a large, gentle owl who perches on the highest branch of the old oak tree. With golden sparks dancing in the air, he takes Stella on an adventure through time — from cave painters to the alphabet, from the lightbulb to learning machines.

But there's a twist. A silver fox appears, promising shortcuts and dazzling towers of crystal. Will Stella recognise what is real from what merely glitters?

Characters

Who you'll meet

Stella

A small girl with a short dark brown bob, a pale blue dress, rosy cheeks and big expressive eyes. Curious, brave, and wonderfully human.

Yakob

A large natural owl with warm brown feathers and amber/gold tones. Big round eyes full of warmth and wisdom. No glasses, no accessories — just presence.

The Silver Fox

Sleek, silver-furred, with a smile just a little too wide. Offers shortcuts and crystal towers. Appears pages 17–22 as a lesson in discernment.

About

Why this book?

Children are growing up in a world where AI is everywhere — and often, the adults around them are just as confused. Stella & Yakob was born from a simple belief: that even the hardest questions can be explored gently, with wonder and honesty.

The book doesn't preach or simplify. It acknowledges that there are no easy answers. But it gives children — and the grown-ups who read to them — a language for curiosity, discernment, and the irreplaceable things that make us human.

— Paul Fleury
